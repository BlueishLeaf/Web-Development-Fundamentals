Intel 750 Series 400GB PCIe SSD Review

Our Rig

  • Motherboard: Asus Z170-Deluxe
  • Processor: Intel Core i5-6600K (not overclocked)
  • RAM: Corsair Vengeance 2666MHz, 16GB DDR4
  • Cooler: Corsair H60 liquid cooler
  • PSU: Corsair CX750M
  • GPU: Nvidia GeForce 1080
  • OS: Windows 10 Pro 64-bit

The Good

This drive has very low latency and high sustained throughput. I have consistently seen transfers over a gigabyte per second even though it is in a PCI-e 2.0 x8 slot so has half the bandwidth it could have. Doesn't require a special controller or special drivers in Linux and didn't require a BIOS upgrade to work as a non-boot volume.

This thing is amazing. Have had it for about 1.5 years. Not a single issue. I use this computer to play Games, VR, encode 4K video from my drone, and program. RAID can't touch this thing. Below I listed a general over view of my hardware inside of computer that this SSD is installed to. PCIe SSD is the only way to go, it takes full advantage of the true speed of SSD. SATA ports are used for storage drives.


The Bad

Won't boot on an Opteron. Takes up a PCI-e slot. Runs very hot. Quite expensive.


Should You Buy?

I've tried other PCI-e solutions and had them not work well under Linux. This one works a charm.
